Brain Signal-Based Secure Object Detection with Multimodal Alignment

Abstract

Target localization aims to accurately locate and identify specific objects within an image, which is a core challenge in computer vision. Traditional object localization systems primarily rely on intermediary modalities—such as text descriptions, voice commands, or visual cues—to interpret human intent. However, these modalities offer only an indirect expression of intent, which limits the efficiency of information transmission. This issue is especially pronounced when detailed descriptions of texture and spatial information are required, resulting in higher interaction costs. Brain signals, as a novel modality, eliminate the ambiguity associated with language or visual translation and reduce cognitive load, making it a promising research direction. Nonetheless, brain signals face developmental limitations due to privacy concerns, challenges in decoding accuracy, and significant cognitive variability across individuals. To address these challenges, we introduce BrainLoc, a lightweight, cross-individual brain signal-based object localization model. BrainLoc aligns brain signals with other modalities, such as image and text, to obtain a comprehensive representation. This model effectively locates target objects directly within complex images, combining the precision of traditional localization systems with the convenience of brain-signal-based approaches. Additionally, we incorporate encryption techniques to protect brain data privacy for the first time, greatly alleviating participants’ psychological concerns and laying the groundwork for increased diversity in future study participation. Experimental results demonstrate that BrainLoc has achieved new state-of-the-art performance in brain-based object localization tasks, showcasing significant advantages in both accuracy and convenience.

Our code is available at https://mindloc-sys.github.io/.
